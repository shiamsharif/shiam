The **Six Thinking Hats** framework helps teams reach better decisions by ensuring that a problem is examined from multiple, distinct perspectives, which prevents critical aspects from being overlooked. By deliberately "wearing" different hats, a group can move beyond their standard thinking patterns to create a more **comprehensive and resilient outcome**.

<Image src={sixHatImage} alt="Six Thinking Hats framework diagram" />

Each hat contributes to the decision-making process in a specific way:

## White Hat: Data

This hat grounds the decision in **rational analysis and facts**. It forces the team to look at available data and market trends to ensure the discussion is based on reality rather than just assumptions.

## Yellow Hat: Positivity

Teams use this to identify the **benefits and opportunities** of a decision. It helps highlight the potential revenue or market share a new idea could capture.

## Black Hat: Downsides

This provides a **defensive approach** by focusing on worst-case scenarios and potential risks, such as whether a new product might "cannibalize" existing ones. This makes the final decision more robust by preparing for what might not work.

## Green Hat: Creativity

When the team identifies risks, the green hat allows them to **generate creative solutions** and alternatives. For example, it can be used to brainstorm ways to prototype and test a product&apos;s value before a full launch.

## Red Hat: Emotions

This hat allows teams to incorporate **intuition and gut feelings** into an otherwise purely rational process. It helps the group check how everyone feels about a plan, which can lower anxiety and ensure everyone is aligned with the emotional weight of a high-stakes decision.

## Blue Hat: Process

This hat is used for **facilitation and control**, ensuring the meeting stays productive. The person in this role can shift the discussion between different hats or suggest next steps, such as postponing a final decision until more data is validated.

Ultimately, this tool helps teams reach a "reasonable next step" because it ensures every perspective is heard, allowing the group to arrive at a balanced and well-vetted conclusion.
